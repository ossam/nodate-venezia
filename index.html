お茶と日本のお話
Stories of tea & Japan
Essence of tea
★
Tea ceremony is not really about the tea — it's about being present, together.
茶道はお茶そのものではなく、共にこの瞬間にいること
★
The host prepares for hours. The guest drinks in three sips. The asymmetry is the point.
亭主は何時間も準備し、客は三口で飲む。その非対称性こそが本質
★
In Japan we have a word — ma — meaning the space between things. Silence, pause, emptiness. Tea lives in ma.
日本には「間」という言葉があります。物と物のあいだ、沈黙、余白。茶は「間」に生きる
Wabi-sabi
★
"Wabi" is finding beauty in simplicity. "Sabi" is finding beauty in the marks of time.
「侘び」は簡素さの美。「寂び」は時間の痕跡の美
★
A cracked bowl repaired with gold — we call this kintsugi. The break becomes the most beautiful part.
割れた茶碗を金で継ぐ「金継ぎ」。割れた箇所が最も美しい部分に

★
I live in Nagano, in the mountains. Cold winters, deep snow, very quiet.
長野の山に住んでいます。冬は寒く、雪深く、静か

山でゆっくり暮らすことが、茶と絵への眼差しを形作った

Guiding the guest
Receiving
★
Bow slightly when you receive the bowl.
受け取るとき、軽くお辞儀を
★
Place it on your left palm, support with your right hand.
左手にのせ、右手で支えます
Turning
★
Turn the bowl clockwise twice — about 90° each time.
時計回りに2回、90度ずつ
★
The "front" is the most beautiful side — offered to you out of respect. You turn it away to drink.
正面は最も美しい面。敬意を込めて向けているので、避けて飲みます
Drinking
★
Drink in three sips. The last sip can be a small slurp — it's polite.
三口半で。最後は吸い切るのが礼儀
★
Wipe the rim with your fingers, then turn the bowl back.
飲み口を指で清め、茶碗を元に戻します
所作を説明する
Explaining movements
★
First, I purify the scoop and tea container with this silk cloth — the fukusa.
袱紗で茶杓と棗を清めます
★
I rinse the whisk in hot water to soften and check it.
茶筅通し — 茶筅を湯で温めて確認
★
Two scoops of matcha into the bowl.
抹茶を二杯入れます
★
I pour hot water and whisk in an "M" shape until it foams.
湯を注ぎ、Mの字を描くように点てます
★
In Urasenke, we whisk until the surface is fully covered in fine foam.
裏千家では、細かい泡で表面を覆うまで
★
Every movement has a meaning — it's a form of meditation.
すべての所作に意味がある。一種の瞑想です

Deeper questions
Why so slow?
★
Slowness is the point. It changes how you see the moment.
遅さが本質です。瞬間の見え方が変わります
Why turn the bowl?
★
It's an exchange of respect — the host shows the front to the guest, the guest turns it away to drink from a humbler side.
敬意の交換です。亭主は正面を客に向け、客はそれを避けて飲む
The spirit of tea
一期一会 ICHIGO ICHIE
"One time, one meeting." This moment will never happen again.
和敬清寂 WA · KEI · SEI · JAKU
Harmony · Respect · Purity · Tranquility — the four principles.
